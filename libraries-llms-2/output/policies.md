Team Playbook - Policies

## 1. Ownership and Assignment

Workers are assigned Tasks within Campaigns. Each Task has exactly one owner at a time. If a Task has no owner for more than one business day, the Campaign owner must reassign it.

Tasks marked as BLOCKED must include a blocker note and an expected unblock date.

## 2. Task Lifecycle

Each Task moves through a status lifecycle: PENDING, IN\_PROGRESS, REVIEW, DONE. Only the assigned Worker can move a Task from PENDING to IN\_PROGRESS. Any teammate can request changes during REVIEW, but only the owner can mark it DONE. Tasks may return from REVIEW to IN\_PROGRESS when fixes are required.

Tasks in DONE status must include a short completion summary.

## 3. Campaign Planning

Campaigns group related Tasks under a shared deadline and budget. Every Campaign must define a business objective, success metric, and target audience. Campaign kickoff includes scope, dependencies, and risk notes. Campaign owners review priority every Monday and rebalance work if needed.

Campaigns with external dependencies must maintain a dependency tracker.

## 4. Budget and Approval Rules

Campaign budgets above ten thousand euros require approval from the Finance team. Campaign budgets above twenty thousand euros require Finance and Director approval. Any unplanned spend above one thousand euros requires a written justification. Budget changes must be logged with reason, owner, and effective date. Financial approvals are stored in the Campaign audit folder.

## 5. Quality and Review

All Tasks must be reviewed by a teammate before being marked DONE. Reviewers verify acceptance criteria, test evidence, and rollback notes. Critical defects found in review are tagged HIGH and escalated immediately. If no reviewer is available, the Campaign owner assigns a backup reviewer.

Repeated review failures trigger a retrospective item for process improvement.

## 6. Escalation and Communication

Workers escalate blocked Tasks to the Campaign owner within one business day. If a blocker remains open for two days, notify the area lead. Incidents impacting deadlines must be posted in the team channel within one hour. All escalations should include current status, impact, and next action. Major decisions are summarized in weekly Campaign notes.

## 7. Reporting and Metrics

Each Campaign tracks throughput, cycle time, and completion rate. The team reviews open risks and overdue Tasks during the weekly standup. Monthly reports include budget variance and quality trend summaries.

Metrics are used for improvement, not individual performance ranking.

## 8. Retention and Audit

Task comments, status transitions, and approvals are retained for twelve months. Audit-ready exports are generated at Campaign close.

Any manual data correction must include who changed it and why. Policy exceptions require explicit approval and expiration date.
